Women’s Hygiene Care

Your complete guide for menstrual hygiene, pregnancy care, postpartum hygiene, newborn care and more.

Period Hygiene

Before Your Period

  • Track your cycle and prepare your hygiene kit.
  • Use cotton underwear for breathability.
  • Keep pads/tampons/menstrual cup ready.

During Your Period

  • Change pads every 4–6 hours.
  • Wash hands before and after changing products.
  • Use pH-balanced intimate wash or water.
  • Keep the genital area dry to avoid infection.

After Your Period

  • Wash underwear properly with warm water.
  • Clean your menstrual cup thoroughly.
  • Note flow changes for future tracking.

Pregnancy Hygiene

Pre-Pregnancy

  • Start folic acid supplements.
  • Get screenings and vaccinations.
  • Maintain oral hygiene.

During Pregnancy

  • Keep the vaginal area clean and dry.
  • Use gentle skincare products.
  • Eat healthy foods & stay hydrated.
  • Avoid unpasteurized foods.

Postpartum Hygiene

After Delivery

  • Use a peri bottle for gentle cleaning.
  • Keep stitches clean.
  • Air-dry nipples after feeding.

C-section Care

  • Keep incision dry.
  • Watch for redness or swelling.

Newborn Care Hygiene

Mother’s Hygiene

  • Wash hands before breastfeeding.
  • Clean breasts with water only.

Baby’s Hygiene

  • Keep cord stump dry.
  • Use mild baby soap.
  • Frequent diaper changes.

UTI & Infection Prevention

  • Drink plenty of water.
  • Avoid holding urine.
  • Wear cotton underwear.
  • Urinating after intercourse prevents bacteria.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to wash hair during periods?

Yes, it is completely safe. Warm water helps relax your body and reduce cramps.

How often should pads be changed?

Pads should be changed every 4–6 hours to maintain hygiene and avoid infections.

Can pregnant women use intimate wash?

Yes, but only mild, pH-balanced, fragrance-free intimate washes recommended by doctors.

What is a normal menstrual cycle length?

A normal menstrual cycle ranges between 21–35 days depending on hormones.

Is white vaginal discharge normal?

Yes, white discharge is normal unless it smells strong or causes itching.

Can stress affect the menstrual cycle?

Yes, stress can delay or disturb periods because it affects hormones.

Is using a menstrual cup safe?

Yes, menstrual cups are safe, eco-friendly and can be worn for 8–12 hours.

Can I exercise during my periods?

Yes, yoga, stretching, and walking help reduce cramps and improve mood.

What foods reduce period pain?

Bananas, nuts, leafy greens, ginger tea and warm foods help a lot.

Is brown period blood normal?

Yes, brown blood means older blood leaving the uterus — completely normal.

Can I swim while on my period?

Yes, with a tampon or menstrual cup. Pads cannot be used for swimming.

Why do I feel tired during periods?

Hormone changes and low iron levels can make you tired.

How can I prevent UTIs?

Drink water, avoid harsh soaps, wipe front-to-back, and change underwear daily.
